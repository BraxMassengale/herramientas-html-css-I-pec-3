The Life of Marcus Aurelius

Marcus Aurelius was born on April 26 in the year 121. He was born into a very influential family with many of his family members holding high governmental positions such as governer, consul, and the next in line for the emperorship.

Throughout his life, Marcus was groomed to become a great politician and social figure. He eventually was put in line to become emperor when he was 17. He ascended to the throne at the age of 40 in the year 161 succeeding the previous emperor Antoninus Pius.

During his reign, Marcus was faced with many challenges such as wars in Syria, Armenia, and Mesopotamia, as well as plagues brought back to heart of rome by the returning armies. Marcus' reign is generally considered to have been very successful and he is counted among the five great emperors.

Though Marcus was a great emperor, he is most well known for his contributions to the Stoic school of thought. During his reign he often journaled his thoughts and reflections for his own personal use. After his death this writings would be discovered and published in what would become known as the Meditations. The writings are of Marcus' musings and thoughts about morality and meaning. This text is considered to be one of the primary examples of Stoic philosophy.

Marcus died in 180 at the age of 58.
